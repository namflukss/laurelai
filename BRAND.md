# Laurel — Brand Brief

A one-page brief for a brand/visual designer. What Laurel is, who it's for, how it should
feel, and the cues that make it unmistakably itself.

---

## In one line

**You made the film. Laurel finds where it belongs.**

Laurel is the festival strategist every independent filmmaker wishes they could afford — an AI
that reads your film, finds the festivals most likely to program it, and runs the whole
campaign: deadlines, fees, premiere strategy, all the way to premiere.

## Positioning

Not a SaaS dashboard. Not a hype startup. Laurel is a **trusted producer's rep / a sharp
gallerist** — literate, calm, cinema-adjacent. It earns trust by being honest ("this is a
Tribeca film, not a Cannes one") and by protecting two things filmmakers can't afford to waste:
**their money and their world premiere.**

## Who it's for

Independent filmmakers — often first-timers — who've just finished a film and face a
bewildering, expensive, deadline-ridden circuit alone. Creative, budget-conscious, proud of the
work, anxious about wasting submission fees and mis-timing a premiere.

## Personality

Five adjectives: **discerning · calm · candid · warm · cinematic.**

Laurel is a confidant, not a tool. It speaks plainly, never oversells, and always tells you the
honest read — then the actionable next step.

## Voice

**Do:** calm, specific, confident. Concrete festival names over hedging. State alignment plainly
("This film aligns well with…"). Always end on something the filmmaker can act on.

**Don't:** no hype, no exclamation marks, no emoji in product copy, no marketing clichés
("Elevate", "Seamless", "Unleash", "Next-gen"), no generic tech tone.

## Name & logo

The **laurel** is the idea — selection, honor, the wreath you earn. The name *is* the logo: an
"L" mark + "Laurel" wordmark; on the site, **"Laurel®"** in an italic serif reads editorial. Keep
the mark simple and confident; avoid literal clip-art wreaths.

## Color

**Warm, not cool.** A cream/paper ground and a single confident ember-orange — the color of a
marquee light, not a notification badge. One accent, used sparingly.

| Token | Value | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Canvas | `#F1EFEA` | warm cream page ground |
| Raised | `#F5F3EE` / `#FFFFFF` | panels, cards |
| Ink | `#16140F` | primary text |
| Slate | `#45413A` | secondary text |
| Muted | `#8C8779` | labels, captions |
| Line | `#E7E4DC` | hairlines / borders |
| **Accent (ember)** | `#ED590C` | the one brand accent — CTAs, active states, the berry |
| Accent hi / deep | `#FF7A26` / `#C2410C` | gradient top / pressed + on-tint text |
| Accent tint | `#FDEAD7` | soft highlight behind active items |

Dark mode is a **warm screening-room** charcoal (`#14110D`), never cool slate.

**Rules:** one accent only; desaturate, don't neon. No purple/blue "AI" gradients, no outer
glows. Borders are hairlines; elevation is a soft, background-tinted shadow.

## Typography

- **Display / UI:** a strong humanist grotesk — **Hanken Grotesk** (700–800 for headlines,
  tight tracking). Bold structure, calm hierarchy.
- **Voice (editorial accent):** an italic serif — **Instrument Serif** / Source Serif — for the
  "film-print / call-sheet" register on marketing and emphasis words.
- **Data:** a monospace — **Spline Sans Mono** — for deadlines, fees, fit scores, eyebrows. The
  numbers should read like a spec sheet.

Headlines control hierarchy with weight + the serif accent, not just size. Mono micro-labels are
small, tracked, uppercase.

## Imagery & motifs

- The **laurel** (selection / honor) and the **cinema** (the dark of a theatre, the warm glow of
  the screen, subtle film grain).
- Typographic "posters" over stock photography — the type *is* the art.
- When photography is used: warm, slightly desaturated, cinematic — never bright corporate stock.

## UI principles

- **Curated, not crammed** — whitespace and restraint; it should feel like a festival catalogue.
- **Honesty made visible** — fit scores with reasons, premiere-conflict warnings, "why it fits."
- **Premiere-safe by design** — the submission order protects the world premiere; surface that.
- Tactile, quiet motion (transform/opacity, soft easing). No bounce, no glow, no noise.

## Taglines

- **You made the film. Laurel finds where it belongs.** (primary)
- Every film has a room that's waiting for it. (closing line)
- An AI curator for independent filmmakers.

## Anti-patterns (designer red flags)

Generic tech blue · purple gradients · neon glows · emoji as icons · exclamation marks · dense
SaaS card grids · pure black (`#000`) · "CRM" energy · Inter as the brand face · stocky corporate
photography. If it feels like a tool instead of a confidant, it's wrong.
