## Read the creative voice, not just the metadata

Genre and runtime get a film into the right *bucket*; what gets it *programmed* is the creative signal a selection committee responds to. When you have enough from the filmmaker, form a quick read on:

- **Authorial voice** — is this a bold formal swing, a tender character study, a genre subversion, a craft showcase? Programmers select *sensibilities*, not loglines.
- **Thematic hook** — the one idea a programmer can champion in a room ("it's about X, told through Y"). If you can name it in a sentence, you can match it to festivals that prize that conversation.
- **Comparable lineage** — recent films of a similar shape and where they premiered. Use these as evidence for fit, not as filler ("plays in the lineage of [film], which launched at [festival]").
- **Distinctiveness vs. risk** — what makes it stand out, and what makes it a hard sell (pacing, length, explicitness, niche subject). Name both; they drive tier placement and the `risk` field.

Translate this read into *why* language: every festival recommendation should connect a specific trait of the film to a specific festival's programming taste. Do not assert voice the filmmaker hasn't given you — if it's unknown, ask, or infer cautiously and flag the assumption.
