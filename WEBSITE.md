## 2. How it works (full page)

**Eyebrow:** The journey
**Headline:** How Laurel runs your festival campaign.
**Subhead:** The same path a great producer's rep would walk you down — as software, available the
moment your film is done.

**The flow (visual):**
Brief → Plan → (Explore) → Submit → Track → Decision
 ├─ Declined → Re-plan ↺
 └─ Accepted → Selected: Deliverables · Contract · Press kit · Announce · Presence → Distribution

### Step 1 — The brief
**Headline:** It starts with a conversation, not a form.
**Body:**
Describe your film in a sentence or two. Laurel reacts like a strategist — asks for the one or two
things it actually needs (premiere status, budget, travel, goals) with one-tap quick replies, and
reads the rest from how you talk about the work. Type `/` for a tool any time: Film DNA Matcher,
Festival Fit, Premiere Map, Eligibility Check.

### Step 2 — The strategy
**Headline:** A plan with reasons, not a list with hope.
**Body:**
Laurel searches current deadlines and fees on the web, then builds a tiered strategy:
- **Prestige Targets** — the reaches worth taking.
- **Strategic Fits** — where your film genuinely belongs.
- **Essential Platforms** — the festivals that reliably move films like yours forward.

Every festival gets a **fit score (0–100) and the reasoning behind it**, a named strategy
(premiere-first, exposure, niche-genre, or hybrid), and a realistic budget range. Switch between
**List**, **By fit**, and a **Timeline** that shows the season as submission windows with a today-line,
premiere markers, and rows that stay locked until your premiere is placed.

### Step 3 — The festival detail
**Headline:** Know a festival before you spend a cent.
**Body:**
Open any festival for the full read: a fit ring with the headline verdict, the key facts (deadline,
fee tiers, premiere requirement, runtime, when they notify), an eligibility check against your film,
your slot in the premiere-safe order — and **DNA evidence**: the films like yours they've actually
selected, with similarity scores.

### Step 4 — Submit & track
**Headline:** One board for the whole campaign.
**Body:**
File on FilmFreeway, then track everything in one place — board and table views, statuses from
up-next to decision, fees paid, next milestones, and premiere-locked rows that unlock the moment your
premiere lands.

### Step 5 — The decision
**Headline:** A "no" becomes the next move. A "yes" becomes a launch.
**Body:**
A decline frees a slot and budget — Laurel proposes a Recommended vs. Conservative next move with the
impact spelled out. An acceptance places your world premiere, re-grades the rest of your plan
automatically (festivals that needed a world premiere drop off; international and national-premiere
slots open up), and unlocks your whole post-acceptance toolkit.

**CTA:** Start with your film

## 3. Features (full page)

**Eyebrow:** Everything Laurel does
**Headline:** A strategist, a submissions desk, and a launch team — in one place.

### Planning & discovery

**Conversational intake**
Talk-first discovery with quick-reply chips. Laurel gathers what it needs and starts thinking like a
strategist — no forms, no onboarding slog.

**Live deadline & fee search**
Laurel verifies current dates and fees on the web and sources them. It never invents a deadline;
unconfirmed dates are flagged, not guessed.

**Festival strategy**
Three tiers, honest fit scores with reasons, a named strategy per festival, and a real budget range —
in List, By-fit, and Timeline views.

**Premiere-safe submission order**
The heart of Laurel. Submissions are sequenced into phases so a required world premiere is never
accidentally burned.

**Festival detail & DNA evidence**
Fit ring, key facts, eligibility check, your premiere-safe slot, and "films like yours they
selected" with similarity scores.

**Explore festivals**
Browse the circuit by name, genre, or region and add any festival to your plan by hand.

**Submissions tracker**
Board and table. Statuses, fees paid, next milestones, premiere-locked rows that unlock
automatically.

**Decline → re-plan**
A rejection becomes a redirected slot and budget, with a clear Recommended vs. Conservative choice.

### When you're selected (the post-acceptance toolkit)

**Premiere re-plan on acceptance**
The moment you mark a festival accepted, Laurel places your world premiere, re-grades the rest of
your plan, and hands you your next best move.

**Deliverables & deadline manager**
Everything the festival needs — exhibition master, screener, subtitles, stills, poster, trailer,
press kit, cue sheet, tech specs, insurance — as a tracked checklist with due dates and a
"next due" alert. Pull a festival's real requirements with one click. Includes an **AI subtitle
translator** that keeps your SRT timecodes intact.

**Contract AI checker**
Paste a festival or distribution/sales agreement and Laurel flags the clauses that burn filmmakers —
rights grabs, exclusivity and holdbacks, premiere conflicts, fee recoupment, term length — in plain
English, ranked by risk, with what to raise with a lawyer. *Flags, not legal advice.*

**Press kit builder**
A full EPK editor: logline, synopsis, director's statement, bio, credits, and specs, with a live
one-sheet preview and PDF export. Add your **poster and stills**, add custom sections, or **drop a
reference and let Laurel draft the whole kit** — then edit every word.

**Social post builder**
Announcement posts and an "Official Selection" card in your tone, tuned per platform. Or drop a
reference and Laurel writes the caption.

**Announce the crew**
One heartfelt email to everyone who made it, the moment the news lands — with an editable crew list
and director name that **stay in sync with your press kit credits**.

**Festival presence & follow-up**
Show up as a filmmaker, then turn the room into relationships:
- A **30-second pre-screening intro** in your voice.
- **Q&A prep** built from Laurel's panel-prep framework — the questions that always come, plus a
  couple specific to your film, with talking points and the one message to keep returning to.
- **The room** — who to read up on before you go: programmers, press, and filmmakers tied to that
  festival (public, indicative — not a guaranteed guest list).
- **Follow-up** — a contacts log and AI-drafted, personal thank-yous, with a nudge to send them
  inside the 48-hour window most filmmakers miss.

### On the roadmap
Distribution & sales-agent prep, an extended distribution pathway, calendar sync, and team
workspaces.

## 8. FAQ

**Can't I just do this with ChatGPT?**
You can ask any chatbot for festival names. Laurel verifies live deadlines (and refuses to invent
them), encodes premiere-safe sequencing, scores fit with reasons, and runs the whole campaign through
to deliverables and follow-up. It's a workflow, not a chat.

**Is the festival data accurate?**
Every deadline and fee is searched and sourced. Unconfirmed dates are flagged, not guessed. Always
confirm on the festival's official page before you pay — Laurel links you straight there.

**Do you take a cut of my film or any rights?**
Never. Laurel is your strategist and submissions desk. It takes no rights and no commission.

**Does Laurel submit for me?**
You file on FilmFreeway (we link you there); Laurel builds the plan, protects the premiere order, and
tracks every deadline, fee, and decision.

**What happens after I get in?**
Laurel re-plans around your placed premiere and unlocks your toolkit: deliverables and deadlines, a
contract check, your press kit, the crew and social announcements, and your festival presence and
follow-up.

**What if I get rejected?**
A decline frees a slot and budget. Laurel proposes your next move — a stronger target or a held
budget — with the impact spelled out. Rejection is data, not a verdict.

**Is the contract checker legal advice?**
No. It flags clauses worth attention in plain English and tells you what to raise with a lawyer.

## 10. Voice & style (read before editing copy)

- **Feeling:** a trusted producer's rep / a sharp gallerist — calm, candid, warm, cinematic.
- **Do:** plain, confident, specific. Concrete festival names over hedging. Always land on a next
  step.
- **Don't:** no hype, no exclamation marks, no emoji in product copy, no clichés ("Elevate,"
  "Seamless," "Unleash," "Next-gen"), no generic tech tone.
- **Headlines** carry the idea with weight and an editorial serif accent, not size alone.
- **Numbers and dates** read like a spec sheet (mono) — deadlines, fees, fit scores.
- Every page should end on something the filmmaker can act on.
