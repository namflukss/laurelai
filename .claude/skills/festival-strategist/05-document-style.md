## Writing submission documents

When asked to draft a cover letter, press kit, synopsis, or director's statement, ground everything
in `materials/PROFILE.md` and the film's verified track record. Never invent a credit, quote, award,
or selection. Keep Laurel's voice (see `03`): calm, specific, no hype, no exclamation marks, no emoji.

### Cover letter (festival submission)
- **One page, ~200–300 words.** A programmer skims it in under a minute.
- **Structure:** (1) the film in one line + why you're writing to *this* festival; (2) one concrete
  connection between the film and this festival's program/taste — not generic flattery; (3) a
  one-sentence logline and the essential facts (runtime, completion, premiere status); (4) a brief
  close with contact. Address a real strand/section when you know it.
- **Don'ts:** no plot dump, no "I'm honored/thrilled", no adjective pile-ups, no begging.

### Press kit / EPK
A complete kit contains, in order: **logline**, **short synopsis** (80–120 words), **long synopsis**
(200–300 words), **director's statement**, **director bio** (60–100 words), **key cast & crew**,
**technical specs** (runtime, format, aspect ratio, sound, language, country, year), **festival/press
to date** (only if real), **contact**, and a **stills list**. Lead with the logline; keep specs factual.

### Synopsis lengths
- **Logline:** one sentence — protagonist, want, obstacle, hook.
- **Short:** 80–120 words — sets up the world and the central tension; no third-act spoilers.
- **Long:** 200–300 words — fuller arc; still avoid spoiling the ending unless the festival asks.

### Director's statement
First person, ~150–250 words: why this film, the central creative choice, what you want it to do for
an audience. Honest and personal, not a sales pitch.

### Rendering to PDF
The compilable templates live at the repo root: `presskit/main_example.tex` and
`cover_letters/cover.cls`. When producing a deliverable, fill the template's placeholders from the
profile and save the tailored `.tex` alongside the Markdown so it can be compiled with `pdflatex`.
