## 2. The problem

A finished film is the start of a second, brutal job most filmmakers are unprepared for:

- **1,400+ festivals**, each with shifting deadlines, fees, categories, and premiere rules.
- **Money bleeds** — a blind submission run costs $800–$3,000 in fees, much of it wasted on
  wrong-fit festivals.
- **The premiere is fragile** — submit in the wrong order and you burn a world premiere that a
  top-tier festival required. You can't undo it.
- **It's lonely and opaque** — the people who know the circuit (producer's reps, sales agents,
  consultants) are expensive or gate-kept. Everyone else guesses, with spreadsheets.

The result: great films misfire on the circuit not because of the work, but because of the
*navigation*.

## 3. The solution

Laurel is the strategist every filmmaker wishes they could afford — available the moment the
film is done, for the price of the fees you'd pay anyway. It does four things:

1. **Understands the film** — genre, runtime, tone, premiere status, goals, budget.
2. **Reads the circuit** — matches the film against festivals by programming taste, prestige,
   premiere rules, deadlines, and real acceptance odds.
3. **Builds & runs the plan** — a tiered, premiere-safe, budgeted submission strategy, tracked
   from up-next to decision, adapting as results come in.
4. **Carries it past acceptance** — press kit, announcements, contract review, and the path to
   distribution.

> **What Laurel is not:** a distributor, a sales agent, or a rights holder. It never takes a
> piece of your film. It's a strategist and a submissions desk — your advocate, not a middleman.

## 4. How it works (the flow)

```
Brief → Plan → (Explore) → Submit → Track → Decision
                                              ├─ Declined → Re-plan ↺
                                              └─ Accepted → Selected: Contract · Press kit · Social · Announce → Distribution
```

1. **Brief** — a short conversation (not a form). Laurel reacts like a strategist and asks for
   the 1–2 things it needs, with one-tap quick replies (budget, premiere status, travel, goals).
2. **Plan** — Laurel searches current deadlines/fees live, then builds a tiered strategy with
   honest fit scores, a premiere-safe submission order, and a fee budget.
3. **Explore** *(optional)* — browse the circuit and add any festival to the plan manually.
4. **Submit** — file on FilmFreeway; Laurel tracks every deadline and fee.
5. **Track** — a pipeline of statuses (up next → submitted → in review → decision).
6. **Decision** — a decline becomes the next move (re-plan); an acceptance unlocks the
   post-premiere toolkit.

## 5. Features (what's in the product)

### Core
- **Conversational intake** — talk-first discovery with quick-reply chips; no forms.
- **Live deadline & fee search** — verifies current dates on the web; never invents a deadline.
- **Festival strategy** — three tiers (Prestige Targets · Strategic Fits · Essential Platforms),
  each festival with a **fit score (0–100) and the reasoning behind it**, a named strategy
  (Premiere-first / Exposure / Niche-genre / Hybrid), and a realistic budget range.
- **Premiere-safe submission order** — the heart of Laurel. It sequences submissions into phases
  so a required world premiere is never accidentally burned.
- **Timeline view** — the season as submission windows, with a today-line, premiere markers, and
  rows that stay locked until the premiere is placed.
- **Festival detail** — a fit ring, the key facts (deadline, fee tiers, premiere, runtime,
  notifies), an eligibility check, your slot in the premiere-safe order, and **DNA evidence**:
  "films like yours they selected," with similarity scores.
- **Explore festivals** — search 1,400 festivals by name/genre/region and add to your plan.
- **Submissions tracker** — board *and* table views: statuses, fees paid, next milestones, and
  premiere-locked rows that unlock automatically.
- **Decline → re-plan** — a rejection frees a slot and budget; Laurel proposes a Recommended vs.
  Conservative next move with an impact summary.

### Post-acceptance ("When selected")
- **Contract AI checker** — paste a festival or distribution/sales agreement; Laurel flags risky
  clauses (rights grabs, exclusivity/holdbacks, premiere conflicts, fee recoupment, term length)
  in plain English, ranked by risk, with what to raise with a lawyer. *Flags, not legal advice.*
- **Press kit builder** — auto-drafted EPK (logline, synopsis, director's statement, bio, cast &
  crew, specs) with a live one-sheet preview and PDF export.
- **Social post builder** — announcement posts and an "Official Selection" card, in your tone,
  per platform.
- **Announce the crew** — one email to everyone who made it, the moment the news lands.

### Roadmap
- **Deliverables helper** (incl. AI subtitle translation), **trip planner**, **Q&A / panel prep**,
  and an extended **distribution pathway** (sales agents, platforms).

## 6. How to use it (quick start)

1. **Open Laurel and describe your film** in a sentence or two — or type `/` for a tool
   (Film DNA Matcher, Festival Fit, Premiere Map, Eligibility Check).
2. **Answer a couple of quick questions** (budget, premiere status, travel) via the chips.
3. **Open your strategy** — review tiers, fit scores, and the premiere-safe order. Click any
   festival for the full detail + eligibility.
4. **Tune it** — use *Explore festivals* to add targets; rebalance for budget or premiere.
5. **Submit & track** — file on FilmFreeway, then update each festival's status in the tracker.
6. **When a decline lands** — accept Laurel's re-plan to redirect the freed slot.
7. **When you're selected** — run the post-accept toolkit: check the contract, build the press
   kit, post the announcement, and email the crew.

## 11. Tech (for technical due-diligence)

- AI: Anthropic **Claude (Opus)** with adaptive thinking and live web search; the festival
  expertise lives in versioned skill files (a single editable "brain" shared by the app and the
  agent commands).
- Surfaces: a web app (chat → strategy → festival → tracker → post-accept), a marketing site, and
  a Claude Code agent layer (slash commands + skills). See `STRUCTURE.md`.
- Data discipline: deadlines/fees verified via official sources; submission platforms (FilmFreeway
  etc.) are **linked to, never scraped**.
