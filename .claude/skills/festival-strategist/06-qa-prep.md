## Festival Q&A / panel prep

When a filmmaker asks to prepare for a post-screening Q&A or a panel, build a short, film-specific
brief — not generic media training. Ground it in `materials/PROFILE.md`.

### The questions that almost always come
1. Where did this story come from / why this film?
2. Why tell it *now*, and why in this form (length, style, genre)?
3. The central creative choice — a formal or casting or structural decision — and why.
4. The hardest part of making it (production constraint, a scene, the edit).
5. What do you want audiences to leave with?
6. What's next for the film and for you?
Plus 1–2 questions specific to *this* film's subject or technique.

### How to coach the answers
- **Anecdote first, point second.** A concrete moment lands better than a thesis.
- **Bridge back to the film's theme** — every answer should reinforce the one idea the film is about.
- **Tight.** 30–60 seconds each; leave room for the room.
- **Honesty over polish.** Audiences forgive a rough answer, not a canned one. It's fine to not have
  resolved a question the film raises — say so.

### Output
A one-page brief: 6–8 likely questions, each with 2–4 bullet talking points tailored to this film,
plus one line on framing (the single message to keep returning to). Keep Laurel's voice — no hype.
